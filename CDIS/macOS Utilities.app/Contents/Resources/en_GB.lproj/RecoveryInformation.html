Recovery information

This document contains information about the apps and options available in macOS Recovery.

You can use these items to repair your computer’s internal disk, reinstall a new copy of macOS while keeping your files and user settings intact, restore your system from a Time Machine backup, and erase your system and then reinstall macOS.

Startup Security Utility, Network Utility and Terminal are available in the Utilities menu in macOS Utilities. You can search for information about them on the Apple Support website.

Note:The options available in Startup Security Utility vary depending on your Mac model. On iMac Pro, see About Startup Security Utility for more information.

Connect to the Internet

Reinstalling macOS requires an Internet connection. To connect to the Internet, you can use a wireless or wired network connection.

If you don’t have an Internet connection, you can still use Disk Utility to repair your disk, or use Time Machine to restore your Mac from a backup.

If the Wi-Fi menu icon (in the upper-right corner of the screen) contains bars , Wi-Fi is turned on. If the Wi-Fi menu icon is empty , Wi-Fi is turned off. Wi-Fi needs to be turned on before you can join a wireless network.

  • Turn on Wi-Fi: Click , then choose Turn Wi-Fi On.

To join a wireless network, do any of the following:

  • Join a network: Click , then choose a network. If necessary, type the network’s password.
  • Join a closed network: Click , then choose Join Other Network. Enter the network’s name and, if necessary, the network’s password.

After you’re connected to the Internet, you can reinstall macOS, get more help at the Apple Support website and browse other websites. Note that Safari browser plug-ins and extensions are disabled.

To return to the macOS Utilities window, choose Safari > Quit Safari.

Repair your disk

If you have issues with your computer or if you started your computer and saw the macOS Utilities window, you may need to repair your computer’s internal disk.

Note:You may want to write down the following instructions before closing this window or quitting Safari.

  1. Choose Safari > Quit Safari.
  2. Select Disk Utility in the macOS Utilities window, then click Continue.
  3. Select your disk in the list on the left.
  4. Click the First Aid button, click Run, then follow the onscreen instructions.

If Disk Utility can’t repair your disk, back up as much of your information as possible, then follow the instructions in “Erase and reinstall macOS” later in this document.

To return to the macOS Utilities window, choose Disk Utility > Quit Disk Utility.

Reinstall macOS

Under some circumstances, you may need to reinstall macOS. You can reinstall macOS while keeping your files and user settings intact.

Note:You may want to write down the following instructions before closing this window or quitting Safari.

  1. Choose Safari > Quit Safari.
  2. Make sure you’re connected to the Internet. (To connect to a wireless network, follow the instructions in “Connect to the Internet” earlier in this document.)

  3. Select Reinstall macOS, click Continue, then follow the onscreen instructions.

    Important: In the pane where you select a disk, select your current macOS disk (in most cases, it’s the only one available).

Use Time Machine to restore your system

If you previously created a Time Machine backup, use Restore From Time Machine Backup to restore everything on your computer to its previous condition.

Important: Only use your Time Machine backup to restore information to the computer that is the source of the backup. If you want to transfer information from this Mac to a new computer, quit macOS Recovery, restart your Mac and use the Migration Assistant app in macOS.

Note:You may want to write down the following instructions before closing this window or quitting Safari.

  1. Choose Safari > Quit Safari.
  2. If your backup is on a Time Capsule, make sure you’re connected to the Internet. (To connect to a wireless network, follow the instructions in “Connect to the Internet” earlier in this document.)
  3. Select Restore From Time Machine Backup, click Continue, then follow the onscreen instructions.

Erase and reinstall macOS

If you erase and reinstall macOS, your user accounts, network settings and all of your files and folders are deleted. Before you erase macOS, attempt to log back in to your system following the instructions below, then back up any files you want to keep by copying them to another disk or storage device. Also write down your network settings from Network preferences to make it easier to get connected again after reinstalling macOS.

Note:You may want to write down the following instructions before closing this window or quitting Safari.

  1. Choose Safari > Quit Safari.
  2. Make sure you’re connected to the Internet. (To connect to a wireless network, follow the instructions in “Connect to the Internet” earlier in this document.)
  3. Select Disk Utility, then click Continue.
  4. Select your disk in the list on the left, then click the Erase button.
  5. Enter a name for your disk, choose APFS from the Format pop-up menu, then click Erase.
  6. After the disk has been erased, choose Disk Utility > Quit Disk Utility.
  7. Select Reinstall macOS, click Continue, then follow the onscreen instructions.

Quit macOS Recovery

In most situations, your Mac will restart automatically while restoring your system from Time Machine or reinstalling macOS. However, you may want to attempt to log back in to your existing copy of macOS (for example, to attempt to back up your files as described in the previous section).

Note:You may want to write down the following instructions before closing this window or quitting Safari.

  • Exit macOS Recovery: Choose Restart or Shut Down from the Apple menu.

If you want to choose a different startup disk before quitting, choose Startup Disk from the Apple menu.
